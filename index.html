Colors of the World

Summary

The main goal of this project is to create suitable life conditions for endemic plant species including "Astragalus beypazaricus", "Ayashensis" and "Salvia aytacnii" at convenient regions of Bilkent University to promote their growth by taking advantage of technology. The plan involves establishing greenhouses on appropriate lands to enable the spread of these plants. The necessary conditions for their cultivation and survival will be thoroughly researched of the selected endemic plants. Greenhouses will be constructed, and technologies such as artificial intelligence and remote access control will be utilized to adjust environmental factors like humidity, temperature, and irrigation to meet the plants' needs. The project will be carried out by a club named "Colors of the World," with student members dividing the tasks. With this project, we aimed to protect and contribute to plant biodiversity, especially endemic ones, as they are at risk of extinction.

Greenhouse Technology

The implementation of cutting-edge greenhouse technology lies at the core of our project, enabling us to create optimal conditions for the cultivation and preservation of endemic plant species. Our greenhouses will be equipped with advanced systems, including artificial intelligence, remote access control, and environmental monitoring. Through the integration of AI, we can analyze and adjust crucial environmental factors such as temperature, humidity, and lighting, ensuring the ideal growth environment for the plants. Remote access control allows for seamless management and monitoring of greenhouse operations, enhancing efficiency and enabling real-time adjustments when necessary. Environmental monitoring systems continuously track and assess various parameters, providing valuable data for informed decision-making and proactive measures. By harnessing these innovative technologies, we strive to achieve the highest level of precision, sustainability, and success in our greenhouse project.
